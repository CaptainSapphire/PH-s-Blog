# Girls Who Code Apollo Meetup 
About a month ago, Girls Who Code hosted their meetup in LA with Apollo; a speed networking event that provides any 18+ member of the Girls Who Code community to network and learn! While the event was a month ago, I will recap the events to the best of my ability. 

## Round 1
The first round was discussion, a more general discussion exploring the workforce. Questions like "How can someone make their experience feel relevant, even if they are still early in their career?" arose. 

## Round 2
Round 2 explored tech roles in the day to day, the round title being "exploring tech roles beyond the obvious." We were advised to look for jobs through university career fairs, which I made note of since that seems to be a theme. 

## Round 3
Round 3 was my favorite round by far, since we went over our LinkedIns and resumes: I was given extensive advice on how to improve my resume, such as :
- add more context, and less listing of activities, get more specific
- Soft/hard skills and tools should be listed
As for my linkedin, I was told to focus on the first impressions of my page, since that is what counts heavily into if a person will be interested in my profile further. I recently updated my resume with these critiques in mind. 

## Round 4
Round 4 was about interviewing, and the major tips was:
- the interviewer will be asking "do I want to work with this person?"; keep in mind the personalities
- Ask questions about the problem given
- "tell me about yourself" include a hobby or personality
- Uniqueness of background provides value
